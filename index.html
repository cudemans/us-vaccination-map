This is how the vaccine is being rolled out across the US

The US has has begun vaccinating its population against COVID-19 en masse. Nearly 30% of the country's more than 330 million residents have received at least one dose. Some states are doing better than others. Those with a darker shade of green have higher vaccination rates. Those in light grey haven't released vaccination data.

Updated: March 29, 2021

The US is administering nearly 3 million doses a day. The country's Food and Drug Administration has so far approved vaccines from Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna, and Janssen. The number of vaccine doses being administered have nearly doubled since late February.
